Our Syllabus

We can teach you on any level up to JLPT N3.
Each course consists of:

  • 10 lessons 
  • 2 hours each 
  • 1 lesson a week, overall 10 weeks or 2.5 months 
  • At the end of a level, you will be taking a JLPT mock exam.

Our Courses

Basic 1 (Lesson 1-10)

Absolute beginners with no prior Japanese knowledge are welcome to try Basic 1. You'll be taught to introduce yourself, describe people, things and places, characteristics, likes and dislikes of a person, and basic actions.

  1. Hiragana, Katakana Writing
  2. Greetings
  3. Identifying People, Things, Places
  4. Self-Introduction
  5. Numbers
  6. Adjectives
  7. Time and Date
  8. Basic Verbs
  9. Existence of Objects and People
View Classes

Basic 2 (Lesson 11-20)

Here, you'll learn about 3 groups of verbs, and their various forms (て,ない,る,た), counting and comparing things, stating your intentions, forming casual sentences and preparing to pass the JLPT N5 mock exam.

  1. Japanese Counters
  2. Comparison
  3. Intention
  4. Verbs (て-form, ない-form)
  5. Adverbs
  6. Verbs (る-form, た-form)
  7. Polite vs Casual sentences
  8. JLPT N5 Revision and Mock Exam
View Classes

Intermediate 1 (Lesson 21-30)

Dialogues, thoughts, giving directions, information in more details, are taught here. You’ll start using advanced grammar terms such as if, even if, can, doing things for someone, know when to use transitive or intransitive verbs, and how to speak respectfully to superiors.

  1. Direct & Indirect Speech, Thoughts and Probably, Maybe
  2. When(とき), Directions(と)
  3. Describing Nouns
  4. State of a situation or activity(こと)
  5. If and Even If
  6. Ability (できます)
  7. Giving and Receiving, Showing Appreciation
  8. Transitive and Intransitive Verbs
  9. Japanese Honorifics
View Classes

Intermediate 2 (Lesson 31-40)

More advanced forms and grammars are taught here. Another way to say if, considerations, giving out instructions, and many ways to reason. We’ll be preparing you for the JLPT N4 Mock Exam too.

  1. Conditional Form
  2. Volitional Form
  3. Imperative and Prohibitive Form
  4. Reasoning (んです)
  5. Deciding (にします)
  6. While (ながら)
  7. Do many things (たり）
  8. Reasoning (し),(て-form),(ので)
  9. For the purpose of (のために)
  10. Usages of ように
  11. Passive Verbs
  12. Indirect Questions
  13. ています/てしまいます/てきます
  14. JLPT N4 Revision and Mock Exam
View Classes

Advanced (Lesson 41-50)

Lastly, we teach another set of advanced grammars here, such as causative verbs like make or let someone do something, or the other way round, and alternatives of honorifics and humble verbs.

  1. Beforehand, Keep (ておきます)
  2. Way of Existence (てあります)
  3. Try (てみます)
  4. Difference between こと and の
  5. Despite/Used in order to (のに)
  6. Heard that, seems like, looks like (そうです、らしいです、ようです)
  7. Looks … (そうです)
  8. Easy or difficult to do (やすい、にくい)
  9. Too much (すぎます)
  10. Time of Action (ところです、ばかりです)
  11. Supposedly (はずです)
  12. Causative Verbs
  13. Alternative Honorific Verbs and Humble Verbs
